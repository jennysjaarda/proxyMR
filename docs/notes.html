1 Meta-analysis across sexes

There are two inputs into MR:

  1. SNP/exposure effects.
  2. SNP/outcome effects.

If we want to perform a MR between exposure \(X\) and outcome \(Y\) comprising both sexes, we have three options:

  1. Use joint GWAS statistics (i.e. \(X \sim G\) is run in sexes combined, adjusted for sex).
  2. Use sex-specific GWAS statistics and then meta-analyze before running the MR.
  3. Use sex-specific GWAS statistics and then meta-analyze the after running the MR.

In other words, there are three options for pooling the data:

  1. At the selection of subgroup for GWAS analysis level.
  2. At the SNP/exposure and SNP/outcome level.
  3. At the MR level.
